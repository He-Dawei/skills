# Pricing Strategy — Korean Translation Services

## Base Formula

```
Price = max(30, chars / 1000 * rate) * rush_multiplier
```

| Direction | Rate (RMB/千字) | Why |
|-----------|-----------------|-----|
| 韩→中 | 50 | Lower: more Korean-to-Chinese translators available |
| 中→韩 | 80 | Higher: fewer native-like Korean writers, charge premium |
| 英→中 | 60 | Medium: common pair, competitive |
| 韩→英 | 70 | Niche: rare combo, charge more |

Minimum: 30 RMB/order (covers setup cost).
Rush fee: x1.5 (< 2 hours delivery).

## Fiverr Pricing (USD)

| Tier | Words | Price | When to Use |
|------|-------|-------|-------------|
| Basic | 100 | 5 | Hook: cheap entry to get reviews |
| Standard | 500 | 15 | Sweet spot: profitable, still competitive |
| Premium | 1000 | 30 | High-value clients, includes formatting |

Note: Fiverr takes 20%. Net on $15 = $12.

**After 5 orders:** Raise Basic to $10, Standard to $20, Premium to $40.

## Market Reference (2026.06)

Search "Korean translation" on Fiverr:
- Lowest: $5/100 words
- Median: $15-25/500 words
- Top sellers: $50-100/1000 words

Competitive advantage: Native Chinese + formal Korean education = better quality than pure machine translation, cheaper than professional agencies.

## When to Raise Prices

| Trigger | Action |
|---------|--------|
| 5 completed orders | +50% across all tiers |
| 10 reviews, all 5-star | Double prices |
| More inquiries than capacity | Raise prices weekly until demand matches capacity |
| Competing on time not quality | Wrong — raise prices, compete on quality |

## When to Discount

| Scenario | Discount |
|----------|----------|
| Repeat client, 3rd+ order | 10-20% off |
| Bulk order (5000+ chars) | 20% off |
| Referral from existing client | 10% off for both |
| Slow month, need cash | 20% off for 48hr flash sale |

Never discount below: 30 RMB/order (Fiverr) or 20 RMB/order (闲鱼).
Below that, your time is better spent on micro-tasks.
